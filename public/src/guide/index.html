Modernized Home Gardening Guide

Learn how to plant fruits, vegetables, lentils, and herbs in containers or sacks, perfect for modern home gardening.

Planting Fruits in Containers

  • Fruit Types: Choose dwarf varieties like strawberries, tomatoes, and citrus trees.
  • Soil: Use a well-drained potting mix with added compost.
  • Watering: Water frequently but avoid waterlogging.
  • Fertilization: Use slow-release fertilizer every 2-4 weeks.

Growing Vegetables in Containers

  • Vegetable Choices: Ideal vegetables include tomatoes, cucumbers, lettuce, spinach, and radishes.
  • Container Depth: Use deeper containers for root crops like carrots.
  • Soil: Use a high-quality potting mix with compost.
  • Watering: Water when the top inch of soil is dry.

Planting Lentils in Sacks

  • Soil: Lentils grow best in well-drained, loamy soil.
  • Sacks: Use fabric grow bags or sacks with good drainage.
  • Watering: Water sparingly, keeping the soil moist during flowering.
  • Support: Use small trellises or stakes for support.

Planting Herbs and Seeds in Containers

  • Herbs: Plant herbs like basil and mint in small containers.
  • Soil: Use a lightweight potting mix with good drainage.
  • Watering: Keep the soil moist during germination.
